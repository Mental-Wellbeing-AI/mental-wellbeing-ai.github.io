Privacy Policy

Mental · Last updated June 2026

Mental is a private mood journal. The short version: your entries live on your device and your own iCloud — we can't read them. We don't ask you to create an account, and we never sell your data. Below is the detail.

What stays on your device

Your check-ins — the mood you log, any note you write, and your tags — are stored on your device and synced through your own private iCloud (Apple's CloudKit). That iCloud data is tied to your Apple ID; we have no access to it and no copy of it on any server we run. Mental has no login and collects no name, email, or account.

Analytics

To understand how the app is used and fix problems, we use PostHog for product analytics. It records anonymous usage events (for example, that a check-in started, a streak milestone was reached, or a screen was opened) tied to a random device identifier — not to your identity.

Analytics never include the text of your notes, your name (only the length of the name you entered is recorded), or your mood entries' content. Analytics are on by default and you can turn them off at any time in Settings.

Apple Health (optional)

If you connect Apple Health, Mental can save your check-ins to Health as State of Mind, and read your sleep, steps, and daylight to show alongside your journal. This stays on your device and is governed by Apple's Health permissions, which you control and can revoke any time.

AI features (optional)

Mental's daily reflections and tag suggestions are generated by an AI service, OpenAI. When you turn on Summaries or Tag extraction, the text of the relevant check-in note is sent to OpenAI solely to generate that content, and only then. It is sent without your name or account, is not stored by us, and is not used to train AI models (per OpenAI's API data policy, content may be retained by OpenAI briefly for abuse monitoring). Reflective prompts run entirely on your device and send nothing. You can turn these features off at any time in Settings → AI. AI features aren't available in all regions.

Your control

You can export your full journal as a file, and you can delete all of your data, from within the app at any time. Deleting it on your device removes it from your iCloud sync too.
